### What inspired you to learn how to code?

### What advice do you have for those who want to get into STEM?

Build stuff 💪, whatever you want, do it. A game, a web, a mobile app, try to do it _(but keep it simple please 🙏)_

Don't you know even what you want to build? Download a Text Editor _(VSCode, Sublime Text, Notepad++, Atom...)_ and start learning web development by yourself. [Start with HTML/CSS](https://www.w3schools.com/html/default.asp), you will feel happy to make visual static pages, then dive into programming languages _(JavaScript/Python/Java/C...)_. You can follow a [roadmap](https://roadmap.sh/).

### Do you have any thoughts on work/life balance?

Job in job time. Easy. I love my job and I love programming and computers but I appreciate my time and that is why I prefer remote working 👨‍💻. I like to be in the office but I don't like to commute to work, too much time just walking/driving 🚗.

### Aside from technical skills, what personality traits/characteristics make for an ideal candidate in your role?

You can be the best programmer in the world but you need to work with others, there is no SWE without people, you need to be able to communicate with others:
1. **Willing to learn/teach** 👨‍🏫 : I like tech, a lot, I've been a programming teacher and I enjoy sharing what I know, but also I value what I can learn from others. I'm eager to learn and try new things.

2. **No ego** 🧘 : There are always better approaches from a problem, if you close yourself into your solution you will never learn new things and you will stuck forever in your tiny knowledge. Everyone can have a better idea than me at any given time. As a software developer you need to listen, analyse and take decisions in order to solve problems. Do not underestimate anything or anyone!

3. **Open-minded** 👍: I'm not like you, and you are not like me, I respect that everyone is different and still we are "geek people doing magic stuff" through black screens. I hate discrimination and I want everyone to feel safe and comfortable.
